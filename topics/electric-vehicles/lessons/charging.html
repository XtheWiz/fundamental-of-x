Charging Curves

A DC fast charger does not hold a constant power. Early on, it pushes max current (CC phase); past ~80% state of charge, it tapers down (CV phase) to avoid stressing the cells. That is why the last 20% takes as long as the first 80%.

CC then CV

Constant current: charger pushes max amps; cell voltage rises gradually. Fast charging happens here.

Constant voltage: once cell voltage hits target, current tapers asymptotically. Slower charging, but necessary to prevent overcharge damage.

Key takeaways

  • C-rate = charge current / cell capacity. 1C = full pack in 1 hour. EVs typically peak around 2–3C.
  • Manufacturers spec '10–80% in X minutes' because the last 20% is much slower.
  • Battery temperature matters: cold packs cannot fast-charge (Tesla pre-conditions on the way to a Supercharger).
  • Newer 800V architectures (Porsche Taycan, Hyundai E-GMP) charge faster than 400V at the same C-rate.
