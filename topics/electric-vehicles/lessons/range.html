Range & Efficiency

Range is just (battery energy) ÷ (energy per km). The first is on the spec sheet; the second varies massively with speed, temperature, drag, payload, and HVAC. Highway range can be 30% lower than EPA.

Energy drains

  • Aerodynamic drag — scales with v². Goes from 20% of consumption at 50 km/h to 60% at 120 km/h.
  • Rolling resistance — roughly constant, ~10–15%.
  • HVAC — heating in winter can be 20–30% of pack drain.
  • Auxiliaries — lights, infotainment, computers — small but non-zero.

Try it

Key takeaways

  • Driving 100 km/h instead of 120 can add 15–20% range.
  • Cold weather kills range twice: through cabin heating and through reduced battery capacity.
  • Heat pumps recover some HVAC energy efficiency — Tesla added them in 2020.
  • Real-world range is often 80% of rated. Plan trips for headroom.
