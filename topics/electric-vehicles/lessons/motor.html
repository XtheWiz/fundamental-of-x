Electric Motors

EV motors deliver maximum torque from zero RPM — that is why a Model 3 outdrags most sports cars. The two dominant types are permanent-magnet synchronous (PMSM, most efficient) and induction (cheaper, rare-earth-free).

Torque vs RPM

EV motors operate in two regions: constant-torque (0 → base RPM, torque flat) and constant-power (above base, torque falls as RPM rises). Max acceleration is in the first region; max top speed is in the second.

Key takeaways

  • Internal combustion engines need a transmission to multiply torque at low RPM. EVs do not — one-speed gearbox is enough.
  • PMSM uses rare-earth magnets (neodymium); Tesla switched from induction to PMSM in 2017 for ~5% efficiency boost.
  • Reluctance motors and externally-excited synchronous motors avoid rare earths — used by BMW iX3, Renault.
  • Motor controller (inverter) does the real work: converts DC battery to 3-phase AC, hundreds of times per second.
