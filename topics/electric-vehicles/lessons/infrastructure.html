Charging Infrastructure

AC charging is slow and at home; DC fast charging is for trips. The world is converging on a few plug standards — but slowly. North America is mid-migration from CCS to Tesla's NACS; Europe stays on CCS; China uses GB/T.

AC vs DC

AC — slow (3.3 kW home, up to 22 kW destination). On-board charger converts to DC. Used overnight.

DC — fast (50 kW to 350 kW). Bypasses the car's AC charger entirely. Used on road trips.

The plug wars

  • CCS (Combined Charging System) — dominant in EU + N.America (until ~2024).
  • NACS / Tesla — smaller plug, was proprietary, now opening to all manufacturers in N.America.
  • GB/T — China standard, biggest installed base.
  • CHAdeMO — Japanese, fading; Nissan Leaf was the main user.

Key takeaways

  • Megawatt charging (1.5 MW+) is coming for trucks. 5-min refills for 800-km range trips.
  • Home charging at 7–11 kW covers most daily commuting overnight.
  • Public DC fast charging is the EV experience that most affects whether people switch.
  • Vehicle-to-Grid (V2G) — EVs feeding power back to the grid — is starting commercially around 2024.
