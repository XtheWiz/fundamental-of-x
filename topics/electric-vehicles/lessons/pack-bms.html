Battery Pack & BMS

A modern EV pack is thousands of individual cells in series for voltage and parallel for capacity. They do not all age the same, so a Battery Management System constantly monitors and equalizes them — and watches for runaway thermal events.

What the BMS does

  • Cell balancing: bleed energy from over-charged cells via tiny resistors, or shuttle energy between them. Without it, the weakest cell limits the whole pack.
  • State estimation: voltage + current + temperature → estimate state of charge and state of health (SOH).
  • Safety cutoffs: disconnect the pack if any cell overheats, overcharges, or overdischarges.

Key takeaways

  • Pack voltage = cells_in_series × ~3.7V. Most EVs run 400V or 800V architectures.
  • Pack capacity = cells_in_parallel × cell_Ah. Tesla Model 3 long-range is ~4,400 cells.
  • Cell-to-cell variation grows with age. A good BMS extends pack life by years.
  • Thermal management (liquid cooling) is part of the same system — cells outside 15–40°C degrade faster or shut down.
