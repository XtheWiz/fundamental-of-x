Regenerative Braking

Running an electric motor backwards turns kinetic energy back into electrical energy. The pack catches it. A well-designed EV can recover 60–70% of braking energy — what used to be wasted as heat in friction brakes now extends your range.

Where the energy goes

Kinetic energy = ½ × m × v². Slowing from 100 → 0 km/h on a 2000kg car: ~770 kJ. Regen captures most of it; friction brakes only kick in for the last few km/h or in emergencies.

Try it

Key takeaways

  • One-pedal driving is regen aggressive enough to bring you to a stop without touching the brake.
  • Regen current is limited by what the pack can absorb safely — cold packs accept less, so cold-weather regen is reduced.
  • Pack at 100% cannot absorb energy; regen is disabled at full charge — that is why some EVs keep ~5% headroom.
  • Friction brakes still exist for emergencies (regen cannot stop a panic-stop fast enough) and for parking.
