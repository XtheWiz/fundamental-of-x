Autonomy Stack

Autonomous driving is sensing + perceiving + planning + controlling. Tesla bets on vision (cameras + neural nets, no LiDAR). Waymo, Cruise, and Chinese players run sensor fusion: cameras + radar + LiDAR + HD maps.

The stack

  1. Sensors: cameras (vision), radar (range + speed), LiDAR (precise 3D), ultrasonic (close-range), GPS, IMU.
  2. Perception: turn raw sensor data into a scene — other cars, pedestrians, lanes, signs.
  3. Prediction: where will each detected object be in 1, 3, 5 seconds?
  4. Planning: choose your trajectory considering safety + comfort + traffic rules.
  5. Control: steering / throttle / brake commands.

Key takeaways

  • L2 (Tesla Autopilot, others) = driver assistance; driver still legally in charge.
  • L4 (Waymo) = full self-driving in a defined area (geofenced), no driver needed.
  • L5 (anywhere, no driver) does not exist yet anywhere, despite marketing.
  • Vision-only debate: still unresolved. Tesla bets on cameras + neural nets matching human capability; competitors argue redundancy with LiDAR is required for safety.
