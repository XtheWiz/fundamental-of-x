Battery Cells

A lithium-ion cell stores energy by shuttling lithium ions between two electrodes. The chemistry decides how much energy you get per kg, how fast it can charge or discharge safely, and how long it lasts.

The chemistries

  • NMC / NCA (Tesla older, GM) — high energy density (~250 Wh/kg), moderate cost, fire risk if punctured. Performance-oriented EVs.
  • LFP (BYD, Tesla SR) — lower energy (~180 Wh/kg) but cheaper, safer, longer cycle life. Standard-range EVs and storage.
  • Solid-state — emerging. Higher density, safer (no liquid electrolyte), but still expensive and slow to manufacture.

Key takeaways

  • Trade-off is roughly: energy density vs cost vs safety vs lifetime. Pick two-and-a-half.
  • LFP has nearly taken over budget EVs because cost won out — at the price of range.
  • Solid-state is 'always 5 years away' but seems to actually be arriving (2027–30).
  • Cell shape matters too: cylindrical (Tesla 18650/21700/4680), pouch (LG, BYD blade), prismatic.
