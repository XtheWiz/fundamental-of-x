Polarization

Light is a transverse wave — the electric field oscillates perpendicular to its direction of travel. The orientation of that oscillation is the polarization. Most light is a random mix; polarizers filter it down to one direction.

Malus's law

If polarized light at angle θ to the polarizer axis hits it, the transmitted intensity is I·cos²θ. Aligned → 100%. Perpendicular → 0%.

Key takeaways

  • Sunglasses are polarizers cutting horizontally-polarized glare (reflections from horizontal surfaces).
  • LCDs use polarizers + voltage-controlled liquid crystals to selectively block or pass light at each pixel.
  • Cinema 3D glasses are circular polarizers, one for each eye.
  • 3D scanning, stress analysis, fiber-optic communication — all rely on controlling polarization.
