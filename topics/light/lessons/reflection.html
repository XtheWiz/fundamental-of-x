Reflection

When light hits a surface, some of it bounces back. Angle of incidence equals angle of reflection — the rule that makes mirrors work.

Specular vs diffuse

Specular reflection: smooth surface, parallel rays stay parallel after bouncing. Mirrors, polished metal, still water.

Diffuse reflection: rough surface, rays scatter in every direction. Most matte objects you see.

Key takeaways

  • Angle in = angle out, measured from the surface normal.
  • Specular vs diffuse depends on surface roughness relative to wavelength.
  • Total internal reflection: light trying to leave a dense medium at a shallow angle can be 100% reflected — how fiber optics work.
  • Computer graphics splits the world into specular + diffuse + ambient lighting; same model.
