Lenses & Imaging

A curved piece of glass refracts every ray that hits it. Choose the curvature carefully and parallel rays meet at a single point — the focal point. That's all imaging is.

Focal length and the thin-lens equation

1/object_dist + 1/image_dist = 1/focal_length. Move the object closer than f → image is virtual (a magnifier). Farther than 2f → image is smaller and inverted. Between f and 2f → image is larger and inverted (like a projector).

Key takeaways

  • Cameras, eyes, microscopes, telescopes — all the same thin-lens equation under the hood.
  • Aberration: real lenses bend different colors and different angles slightly differently. Multi-element lenses correct for this.
  • Aperture is the size of the opening; f-number (f/1.4, f/8) is focal length / aperture diameter. Smaller f-number = more light, shallower depth of field.
  • Modern phone cameras use computational tricks (multi-frame, ML-driven) to overcome small-physical-lens limits.
