Refraction

Light slows down in denser materials. The speed change at a boundary bends the ray. Snell's law gives the exact angle.

Snell's law

n1 sin θ1 = n2 sin θ2, where n is the refractive index (1.0 for vacuum, 1.33 for water, 1.5 for glass, 2.4 for diamond).

Try it

Key takeaways

  • Refraction is why a straw in a glass of water looks bent.
  • Different colors bend by slightly different amounts — that is why prisms split light, and rainbows happen.
  • When sin θ would need to exceed 1, you get total internal reflection instead — the basis of fiber optics.
  • Atmosphere has variable n with altitude — mirages, twinkling stars, atmospheric refraction.
