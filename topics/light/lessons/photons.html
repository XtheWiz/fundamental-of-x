Wave-Particle Duality

Light is a wave. Light is also a stream of discrete particles called photons. Both are true, depending on which experiment you run. Welcome to quantum mechanics.

The photoelectric effect

Shine light on a metal. Electrons get ejected — but only above a threshold frequency, and the kinetic energy of each electron depends on frequency, not intensity. That makes no sense for waves, perfect sense for particles. Einstein won the Nobel for this.

The single-photon double-slit

Fire photons one at a time through a double-slit. Each hits a single spot (particle). But over many photons, the fringe pattern emerges (wave). Each photon somehow goes through both slits.

Key takeaways

  • Photon energy = h·f, where h is Planck constant. More frequency, more energy per packet.
  • Lasers, solar cells (next topic!), photoreceptors in eyes, photo film — all depend on the photon picture.
  • Wave-particle duality applies to all matter — electrons, atoms, even small molecules show wave behavior.
  • Quantum mechanics is the framework that handles both pictures consistently. We don't get to pick.
