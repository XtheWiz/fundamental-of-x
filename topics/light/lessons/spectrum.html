The Spectrum

All light is the same physics — oscillating electric and magnetic fields. The difference between radio, visible, and gamma is just wavelength (or equivalently frequency, since c = lambda*f).

What you can see

Visible light is roughly 380–750 nm. Below: ultraviolet → X-ray → gamma. Above: infrared → microwave → radio. Same equations, different scales.

Energy per photon scales inversely with wavelength: a UV photon carries 1000× the energy of an infrared photon. That is why UV burns and infrared just warms.

Key takeaways

  • Visible is a narrow slice. Most of the EM spectrum is invisible to us.
  • Wavelength determines energy and behavior — diffraction patterns, absorption by matter, what materials are transparent vs opaque.
  • Antennas, fiber optics, X-ray imaging, microwave ovens, sunlight — all the same Maxwell equations.
  • Cameras (CMOS) detect 350–1000 nm; phones include IR filters because the sensor would otherwise pick up unwanted heat radiation.
