Grover's Algorithm

You have N unsorted entries and want to find a specific one. Classically: N/2 lookups on average. Grover: √N. For N = 1 million, that's 1000 instead of 500,000 — a quadratic speedup.

The intuition

Put a uniform superposition over all N items. Use an 'oracle' to negate the amplitude of the target item, then reflect all amplitudes around their mean. After O(√N) iterations the target amplitude dominates — measure, and you'll see it.

Try it

Key takeaways

  • Grover gives a quadratic speedup — not exponential. Big for medium problems, less dramatic than Shor.
  • Provably optimal: no quantum algorithm can search faster than √N.
  • Used as a subroutine in many quantum algorithms (e.g., quantum collision finding).
  • Crypto impact: symmetric ciphers need double the key length (AES-256 becomes effectively AES-128 under Grover).
