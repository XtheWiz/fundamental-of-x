Shor & Post-Quantum

Shor's algorithm factors integers in polynomial time — something classical computers cannot do (and RSA's security depends on them not being able to). When large fault-tolerant quantum computers exist, current public-key crypto breaks.

What Shor does

Given N = p × q, find p and q. Classical best: sub-exponential. Shor: polynomial in log(N). Uses the quantum Fourier transform to find the period of a function, which leads to the factors.

Crypto fallout

  • RSA — broken.
  • ECC (elliptic curve) — also broken via a similar discrete-log algorithm.
  • Diffie-Hellman — broken.
  • Symmetric crypto (AES) — just needs bigger keys (Grover, not Shor).
  • Hashes — mostly fine.

Post-quantum crypto

NIST has standardized lattice-based replacements: ML-KEM (Kyber) for key exchange, ML-DSA (Dilithium) for signatures. Already rolling out in TLS, SSH, PGP.

Key takeaways

  • Shor is the headline result — quantum computers can break the math current crypto depends on.
  • But: we need millions of logical qubits with low error rates. Today's machines have ~1000 noisy qubits.
  • 'Harvest now, decrypt later' attacks are already considered — record encrypted traffic today, decrypt when quantum arrives.
  • Post-quantum migration is happening now (years before the threat is real) because the lead time is long.
