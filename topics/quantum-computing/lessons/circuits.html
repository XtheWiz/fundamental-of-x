Quantum Circuits

A quantum circuit is a sequence of gates on qubits. The classic intro circuit: two qubits, one Hadamard, one CNOT — and out pops a Bell pair, the entanglement from lesson 3.

Reading a circuit

Each horizontal line is a qubit; gates are boxes on the lines; time flows left to right. Two-qubit gates connect lines vertically. Measurement boxes at the end turn quantum states into classical bits.

Bell pair recipe

  1. Start with two qubits in |00⟩.
  2. Apply H to the first → (|00⟩ + |10⟩)/√2.
  3. Apply CNOT (first as control, second as target) → (|00⟩ + |11⟩)/√2.
  4. Measure both. You'll get 00 or 11, never 01 or 10.

Key takeaways

  • The Bell-pair circuit is 3 gates and creates the most-used entangled state in the field.
  • Circuits look like sheet music — a small vocabulary, infinite arrangements.
  • Modern quantum programs (Qiskit, Cirq) are libraries that build circuits the same way.
  • Depth (gate layers) matters as much as width (qubit count) for what's achievable.
