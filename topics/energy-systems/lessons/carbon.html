Carbon Accounting

Every kWh has a carbon intensity (gCO2/kWh) that depends on the generators running at that moment. Average intensity is easy to publish but misleading — what matters for any new load is the marginal source, which is usually gas.

Try it

Pick a country and hour. See the live mix, average vs marginal carbon, and what an EV charged then actually costs in CO2.

Key takeaways

  • Average: total emissions ÷ total kWh. Easy. Misleading for choices.
  • Marginal: emissions of the source that ramped to serve your specific kWh.
  • France: avg ~50 g/kWh (nuclear). Wyoming: avg ~700 g/kWh (coal).
  • An EV charged on coal still beats a gasoline car — ICE drivetrain is ~25% efficient end-to-end.
