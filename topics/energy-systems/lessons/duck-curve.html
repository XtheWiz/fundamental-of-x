The Duck Curve

Net load = total demand minus variable renewable supply. In California, rooftop solar has carved such a deep daytime trough that the residual load shape looks like a duck — flat morning, valley at noon, steep evening ramp.

Try it

Slide solar penetration from 0% to 60% and watch the duck appear.

Key takeaways

  • Solar zeros out midday demand → midday wholesale prices crash, sometimes below zero.
  • The evening ramp (3 GW/hr in CA) is the hardest part — sun drops, demand peaks.
  • Batteries soak up the midday glut and discharge at the evening peak. Storage saves the duck.
  • Same shape now appearing in Texas, Australia, Spain — anywhere solar penetration > 15%.
