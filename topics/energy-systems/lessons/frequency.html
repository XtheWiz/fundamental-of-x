Lesson 04

Grid Frequency

AC grids hold frequency at exactly 50 or 60 Hz. If demand exceeds supply, generators slow — frequency drops. Operators have layers of response: rotating inertia first, then governor droop, then automatic generation control, then load shedding.

Try it

Trip a 1 GW generator and watch the cascade: inertia, primary response, secondary.

Key takeaways

  • Frequency is the instant signal of supply-demand balance. ±0.5 Hz is a crisis.
  • Inertia (rotating mass) gives operators seconds to react. Wind and solar have zero inherent inertia.
  • Synthetic inertia from batteries / grid-forming inverters fills the gap on renewable grids.
  • The 2003 NE blackout, the 2019 UK blackout — both started with a frequency excursion.
