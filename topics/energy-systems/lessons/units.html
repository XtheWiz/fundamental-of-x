Units & Scales

The 'capacity' of a power plant is in watts; the 'energy' it produces is in watt-hours. A 1 GW plant running flat-out for an hour makes 1 GWh — but a 1 GW solar array produces maybe 5 GWh in a day, because it does not run all night.

Try it

Convert between units and check capacity factor for different sources.

Key takeaways

  • Watts vs watt-hours: power vs energy. Always check the unit.
  • kWh (a house), MWh (a small factory), GWh (a city day), TWh (a country year).
  • Capacity factor: actual output ÷ max possible. Solar ~22%, wind ~35%, nuclear ~90%.
  • US uses ~4,000 TWh/year. World uses ~28,000 TWh/year and growing.
