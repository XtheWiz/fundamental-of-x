Lesson 08

Latency & Performance

Why does your site feel slow? Almost never CPU. Almost always waiting. The internet has hard physical limits, and understanding where the milliseconds go is half the battle.

The four costs

  • Propagation delay — the speed of light. Roughly 1ms per 100km (in fiber it's actually about 2/3 light speed). Tokyo↔NYC ≈ 100ms RTT minimum.
  • Transmission delay — how long it takes to push the bytes onto the wire at your bandwidth.
  • Processing delay — server CPU, parsing, building response. Usually small.
  • Queuing delay — packets waiting in router buffers. The hidden cause of "bufferbloat."

Plus one cost-multiplier: round trips. Every "wait for a response before sending more" eats one full RTT.

Key takeaways

  • RTT is bounded by physics. You cannot make Tokyo ↔ NYC faster than ~100ms — only fewer trips.
  • Bandwidth scales fine; latency doesn't. Doubling your pipe rarely halves your page load.
  • CDNs win by getting closer, not by being faster. Less distance = less RTT.
  • The single most-effective optimization is fewer round trips: HTTP/2 multiplexing, HTTP/3 0-RTT, compression, batching requests.
