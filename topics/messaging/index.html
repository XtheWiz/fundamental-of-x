Fundamental of Messaging

Two services need to talk. Direct calls couple them — when one is slow, the other waits; when one's down, the other fails. A message broker between them turns synchronous coupling into asynchronous flow, but it brings its own questions: ordering, delivery guarantees, what happens when consumers fall behind.

Kafka is the canonical example, but the patterns apply to RabbitMQ, SQS, NATS, Redis Streams, and every queue/broker in between.
