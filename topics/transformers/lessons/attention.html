Attention

The one idea that powers every modern LLM. Each token in a sentence looks at every other token and decides, "how much of your meaning should I mix into mine?" That mixing, weighted by similarity, is attention.

Q, K, V

From each token's embedding, the model produces three vectors:

  • Query (Q) — "what am I looking for?"
  • Key (K) — "what do I offer?"
  • Value (V) — "if you pick me, here's what you get."

Each Q is dotted with every K to get a similarity score. Softmax over those scores gives weights. The output is a weighted sum of V's. One formula: Attention(Q, K, V) = softmax(QKᵀ / √d) · V.

Why √d?

Without scaling, the dot products grow with dimension d. Large numbers in softmax → near-one-hot output → poor gradients. Dividing by √d keeps the variance stable. Tiny trick, big practical difference.

Try it

A short sentence. Pick a query token; see its attention weights to every other token.

Key takeaways

  • Attention is "weighted soft-lookup": instead of one fixed table, you pick from all candidates softly based on similarity.
  • Cost is O(n²) in sequence length — every token attends to every other. That's why context windows have practical limits.
  • Self-attention: Q, K, V all come from the same sequence. Cross-attention: Q from one sequence, K and V from another (used in encoder-decoder models, image-text models).
  • "Attention is all you need" (2017) — the paper that replaced RNNs. The architecture has barely changed since for LLMs.
