Architectures in Practice

None of the primitives in this topic stands alone. Real systems compose them. Here's the recipe behind four widely-deployed databases — pick one and see which of the previous seven lessons it pulls from.

The composition lens

Every distributed system answers the same handful of questions: how does it pick a leader (or does it)? What consistency does it promise? What does it do during partitions? How does membership work? The answers are the system's identity.

Try it

Pick a system. See its design choices on each axis, with cross-links back to the primitive lessons.

Key takeaways

  • Systems aren't just "SQL or NoSQL" — they're a profile of choices across many axes, and the choices are tradeoffs, not strict orderings.
  • Newer systems (Spanner, CockroachDB) blur the historical CP/AP split by exploiting tight clocks (TrueTime) and clever consensus.
  • Understanding the primitives means you can read any new system's whitepaper and know what to look for.
  • The most expensive mistakes in distributed systems come from using the wrong tool — a CP store for shopping carts (slow, occasional rejection) or an AP store for accounts (lost money).
