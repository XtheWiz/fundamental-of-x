MPPT

A solar panel's current-voltage curve has one point where power (V × I) is highest. That maximum power point shifts with light level and temperature — so the inverter actively hunts for it. This is MPPT, Maximum Power Point Tracking, and it adds 10–30% real-world output vs naive operation.

The IV curve

Short-circuit current is the y-axis intercept; open-circuit voltage is the x-axis. The curve bends sharply in between — and the bend is where power is max. As irradiance and temperature change, the curve and the optimum move.

Hill climbing

Common algorithm: 'perturb and observe.' Try a slightly higher voltage; if power increased, keep going; if it dropped, reverse. Cheap, simple, doesn't need to know panel characteristics.

Key takeaways

  • MPPT is essentially free 10–30% — should be in every inverter.
  • Per-panel MPPT (microinverters, optimizers) helps a lot under partial shading vs string inverters.
  • Modern algorithms add tricks (sweep + perturb) to avoid getting stuck on local maxima caused by shading.
  • MPPT trackers also apply in EV battery charging and other power-electronics contexts.
