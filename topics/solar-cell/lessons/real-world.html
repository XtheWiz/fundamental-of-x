Lesson 08

Real-World Efficiency

A datasheet says 22% efficiency. Your roof never gets that. Why? Temperature, angle, dust, aging, mismatch, inverter losses, soiling, snow. Each percentage point matters when you're paying upfront for 25 years of output.

Loss breakdown

  • Temperature — efficiency drops ~0.4% per °C above 25°C. A hot rooftop runs at 60°C → ~14% loss.
  • Angle/orientation — flat roof gets ~15% less than optimal tilt.
  • Soiling — dust, bird droppings, pollen → 2–5% typical, more in dry climates.
  • Mismatch + string losses — 2–5%.
  • Inverter — ~3% loss in DC→AC conversion.
  • Aging — ~0.5% per year for silicon.

Key takeaways

  • A 22% panel typically delivers 15–17% under real conditions.
  • Cleaning panels is undervalued — 5% extra output for free, often.
  • Bifacial panels (catch reflected light from behind too) add 5–15% in the right environments.
  • These losses are why a system's 'capacity factor' (annual energy ÷ peak × hours/year) is only ~15–25%, not 100%.
