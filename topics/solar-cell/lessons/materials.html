Materials

Different photovoltaic technologies trade off efficiency, cost, weight, manufacturing complexity, and lifetime. Silicon dominates the market because it is good enough and cheap. The frontier is moving toward perovskite-silicon tandems.

The lineup

  • Crystalline silicon — 90% of installed panels. ~22% efficient. Mature.
  • Thin-film (CdTe, CIGS) — cheaper per watt, slightly lower efficiency, lighter.
  • Perovskite — emerging. Cheap to make, tunable bandgap, but degrades fast. Lab record: ~26%.
  • III-V (GaAs etc.) — expensive, very efficient (>30% single-junction). Used in satellites.
  • Tandems — perovskite on silicon. Lab: ~34%. The most likely commercial future.

Key takeaways

  • Silicon is the boring, reliable, cheap workhorse. Hard to beat on $/watt.
  • Perovskite is exciting but lifetime is the open problem — current best ~10 years vs silicon's 25–40.
  • Multi-junction tandems are the future of utility-scale generation if perovskite stability gets solved.
  • Manufacturing scale and supply chain matter as much as efficiency: China's silicon dominance is geopolitical.
